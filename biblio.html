NLP application : Text classification of BBC news dataset
We will be using the "BBC-news" dataset available in Kaggle, firstly, we will pre-process the dataset ( Cleaning, instance selection, normalization, feature extraction, and selection) and then build three types of models to classify sentences into 5 categories of vocabulary ( Tech, business, sport, entertainment, and politics ) secondly, we will compare models performance and visualize word embedding in 2D using PCA and in 3D using T-SNE. The Algebraic Theory of Quadratic Forms - Witt Theory
The Witt theory, named after the work of the German mathematician Ernst WITT (1911-1991), is a fundamental tool in the algebraic theory of quadratic forms. E. Witt is considered as the first mathematician to have studied quadratic forms on any body; before him quadratic forms had been considered on R (Sylvester) and on Q (Minkowski & Hasse). This theory applies to the classification of quadratic forms on an arbitrary field K thanks to the ring and the Witt group W(K). Quadratic forms appear in several domains and one can reduce the classification of some mathematical objects to that of quadratic forms, one can for example use this classification to study conics. When considering the problem of the classification of quadratic forms, we ask the questions: given two quadratic forms on a field K, when are they equivalent? Can we obtain one from the other? These questions are easy when the field is R or C, but they become more difficult in an arbitary field, we introduce here invariants which help to answer them. In this context, an invariant is a mathematical object associated to a quadratic form which does not change when we replace this form by an equivalent one. These invariants are therefore used to distinguish two quadratic forms that are not equivalent . Click here to read the paper.
